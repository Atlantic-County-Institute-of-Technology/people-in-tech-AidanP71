Ivan Sutherland

Ivan Sutherland was born in Hastings, Nebraska in 1938. He spent most of his life with computers where he would go to college to earn his Bachelor's Degree in electrical engineering from Carnegie Mellon University, Master's Degree in electrical engineering from Caltech, and his Doctorate from MIT. After his time in college he went on to be a first lieutenant in the Army where he would serve as a electrical engineer. After his time in the amry he held partnerships at Harvard and Caltech where he would be a cofounder of Sporull and Associates. He served as a vice president for Sun Microsystems Laboratories. In his career he had many oppurtunites and capitalized on them. He won many awards including a Smithsonian Computer World Award, the Kyoto Prize, and many more. Now he spends his time as a Asynchronous Systems researcher at Portland State Univeristy with his wife.
